# Epoxy Pour Calculator

## Purpose
Calculate the exact amount of epoxy resin needed for river tables, bar tops, and coating projects.

## Features

### Volume Calculation Methods
- **Rectangular Pour**: For straight-sided projects (Length × Width × Depth)
- **Circular Pour**: For round projects (π × Radius² × Depth)
- **Custom Area**: Enter pre-calculated area directly

### Inputs Required
- **Length** (inches) - for rectangular pours
- **Width** (inches) - for rectangular pours  
- **Diameter** (inches) - for circular pours
- **Depth** (1/16" to 6" typical pour depth)
- **Waste Percentage** (10-20% recommended for mixing errors and spillage)

### Results Provided
The calculator displays volume in multiple units:
- **Ounces** (for small pours and precise mixing)
- **Gallons, Quarts, Pints** (US standard measures)
- **Milliliters & Liters** (metric measurements)
- **Cost Estimate** (if epoxy price per gallon is configured)

## Using the Calculator

### Step 1: Enter Dimensions
For rectangular pours:
1. Enter **Length** in inches
2. Enter **Width** in inches
3. Enter **Depth** (pour thickness)

For circular pours:
1. Enter **Diameter** in inches
2. Enter **Depth** (pour thickness)

### Step 2: Set Waste Factor
- **0%**: No waste (precise calculation only)
- **10%**: Minimal waste allowance
- **15%**: Standard recommendation
- **20%**: Safe margin for large or complex pours

### Step 3: Review Results
The calculator automatically displays:
- Total volume in all common units
- Estimated material cost (if pricing configured)

## Important Safety Notes

⚠️ **CRITICAL WARNINGS:**

1. **Heat Management**
   - Always mix resin in small batches
   - Large volumes generate excessive exothermic heat
   - Maximum recommended single pour: 1-2 gallons depending on resin type

2. **Deep Pour Considerations**
   - For depth > 2", use multiple thin layers
   - Allow each layer to cure before adding next
   - "Deep pour" epoxy formulations available for thick applications

3. **Work Time**
   - Standard epoxy: 20-45 minute work time
   - Deep pour epoxy: 2-4 hour work time
   - Temperature affects cure speed dramatically

## Tips & Best Practices

### Accurate Measurements
- Measure mold/cavity carefully - irregular shapes need area calculation
- Account for thickness variation in live-edge projects
- Round UP when close to standard container sizes

### Cost Management
- Configure epoxy costs in the **Manage Costs** section (About tab)
- Compare pricing per gallon vs. per kit
- Bulk purchases typically offer 20-30% savings

### Waste Factors by Project Type
- **Flat coatings**: 10% waste sufficient
- **River tables with curves**: 15% recommended
- **Complex molds/irregular shapes**: 20% for safety
- **First-time users**: Start with 20% until experienced

- Dimensions: 48 L × 18 W × 2 D

- With 15% waste: **1.15 gallons** → Buy 2 gallon kit

### Bar Top Coating (72" × 24" × 1/8")

- Area: π × 18² = 1,017.88 sq in
- Volume: 254.5 cubic inches = **0.147 gallons**
- With 15% waste: **0.17 gallons** → Buy 1 quart

## Troubleshooting

### Results Seem Too High
- Verify depth is in inches (not mm or fractions)
- Check waste percentage setting
- For thin coatings, depth should be 0.0625" (1/16") to 0.125" (1/8")

### Results Seem Too Low
- Ensure all dimensions entered correctly
- Add appropriate waste factor
- Remember: 1 gallon = 231 cubic inches

### Cost Not Showing
- Configure epoxy pricing in **About** tab → **Manage Costs**
- Select epoxy brand from dropdown
- Costs auto-calculate when brand selected

## Formula Reference

**Rectangular Volume**: `V = Length × Width × Depth`  
**Circular Volume**: `V = π × (Diameter/2)² × Depth`  
**Custom Volume**: `V = Known Area × Depth`

**Conversions**:
- 1 gallon = 128 fluid ounces = 231 cubic inches
- 1 quart = 32 oz = 57.75 cubic inches
- 1 liter = 1000 ml = 61.02 cubic inches

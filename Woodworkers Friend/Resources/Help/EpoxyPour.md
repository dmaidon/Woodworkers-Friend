# Epoxy Pour Calculator

## Overview
Calculate the volume of epoxy resin needed for river tables, casting projects, and decorative pours.

## Calculation Methods

### Rectangular Pour
For flat tables or boxes:
- Length × Width × Depth = Volume

### Circular Pour
For round tables or cylinders:
- π × Radius² × Depth = Volume

### Custom Shape
For irregular shapes:
- Enter known volume
- Or calculate area first

## Required Inputs

- **Length/Width/Diameter**: Dimensions in inches
- **Pour Depth**: Thickness of epoxy layer
- **Number of Pours**: Total layers needed
- **Waste Factor**: Extra for mixing waste (10-15%)

## Results Provided

- **Volume in cubic inches**
- **Gallons needed**
- **Ounces needed for small pours**
- **Adjusted for waste factor**

## Important Safety Notes

### Heat Management
- **Maximum pour depth varies by product**
- Thick pours generate heat (exothermic reaction)
- Multiple thin pours are safer
- Allow cooling time between layers

### Typical Pour Depths
- **Standard epoxy**: 1/4" - 1/2" per layer
- **Deep pour epoxy**: Up to 2" per layer
- **Check manufacturer specs**

### Mixing Ratios
- Follow product instructions exactly
- Usually 1:1 or 2:1 by volume
- Measure carefully
- Mix thoroughly

## Tips

### Pour Planning
- Calculate each layer separately
- Allow 24-48 hours between pours
- Ambient temperature affects cure time
- Maintain consistent temperature

### Cost Estimation
- Epoxy is expensive
- Calculate accurately to avoid waste
- Order slightly more than needed
- Can't use partially mixed batches

### Common Projects
- **River tables**: Multiple thin pours
- **Coating**: Thin flood coat
- **Casting**: Deep pour formula
- **Filling voids**: Calculate void volume

## Safety Precautions

### Always:
- Work in ventilated area
- Wear protective gear (gloves, respirator)
- Follow manufacturer safety data
- Keep away from heat sources
- Have spill cleanup ready

### Never:
- Mix more than you can use immediately
- Pour thicker than recommended
- Work in unventilated space
- Touch uncured epoxy bare-handed
